Bio

I'm Teemo Cho (they/them), a cosplayer based in San Francisco, CA. I discovered cosplay back in middle and high school, discovering my passion for the hobby with my earliest cosplays in 2015. While only initially using thrifted items, $30, and random materials, I managed to create a handful of cosplays that caught peoples’ attention. My first cosplay was Annie from League of Legends, during a Halloween day in middle school. After finding out that costuming was a hobby I was willing to do, I made DJ Sona’s helmet with cardboard, party streamers, sandpaper, nail polish, and lots of paint. I already had some interest in cosplay even though I never knew that "cosplay" was a widely used term.

Later that year, when entering high school, I received help from family friends to create a Star Guardian Lux cosplay to wear during Halloween that year. That Lux cosplay became a part of my identity throughout the school, as I had emerging fashion interests. Over the next few years, I took a break from cosplaying, but Lux inspired me to keep sewing my own outfits. In 2018, I decided to return to cosplay with Star Guardian Miss Fortune for Halloween 2018 and K/DA Evelynn for a school festival in Spring 2019.

After high school, cosplaying was more of a minor side thing for me, even throughout the pandemic. One day, Riot Games themselves featured them in my Spirit Blossom Yone cosplay, and now that I got peoples' attention on Riot's social media posts, I decided to dedicate my near future to cosplay.

To me, cosplay bridges gaming, fancy outfits, creativity, and an expression of myself.
